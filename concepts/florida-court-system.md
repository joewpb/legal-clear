# Florida Court System

Understanding the Florida court structure is essential for pro se litigants to
know which court to file in and which forms to use.

## Structure

### Supreme Court
- 7 justices, appointed
- Final appeals, rule-making, attorney discipline
- Publishes statewide approved forms (especially family law)

### 5 District Courts of Appeal
- Intermediate appeals
- Each covers multiple circuits

### 20 Circuit Courts
- General jurisdiction trial courts
- Handle: family law (divorce, custody), felonies, civil cases over $50,000,
  probate, guardianship, juvenile cases
- Each circuit covers 1-7 counties
- Pro se filing fee: $300-$414 depending on case type

### 67 County Courts
- Limited jurisdiction
- Handle: misdemeanors, small claims (up to $8,000), evictions, traffic
- Pro se filing fee: $55-$300 depending on case type and county

## Key for Pro Se Litigants

- **Family law, probate, guardianship → Circuit Court** (where you or the
  other party lives)
- **Small claims, evictions → County Court** (where the property is or where
  the defendant lives)
- **Domestic violence injunctions → Circuit Court** (any county, no filing fee)
- **Name change → Circuit Court** (where you live)

## Circuit ↔ County Mapping

Florida has 20 circuits covering 67 counties. Each county belongs to exactly
one circuit. The circuit court for your county determines which local forms
and procedures apply.

See `raw/articles/florida-court-forms-dataset.json` for the complete mapping.

## E-Filing

All courts accept filing through the Florida Courts E-Filing Portal
(myflcourtaccess.com). Pro se litigants can register as "Self-Represented
Litigant" — it's free and works for all counties.
